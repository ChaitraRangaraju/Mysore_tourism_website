Mysore Palace

Mysore Palace, also known as Amba Vilas Palace, is a historical palace and a royal residence (house). It is located in Mysore, Karnataka. It used to be the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty and the seat of the Kingdom of Mysore. The palace is in the centre of Mysore, and faces the Chamundi Hills eastward. Mysore is commonly described as the 'City of Palaces'.


Sri Chamundeswari Temple

Chamundeswari temple is situated on the top of Chamundi hill which is about 3,489 ft. above sea level and located at a distance of 13 Kms from Mysore. Halfway to the summit is statue of bull Nandi, the vahana of Lord Shiva. The temple is dedicated to Sri Chamundeswari, the titular deity of the Mysore Royal Family also described as ‘Mahishasura Mardini’ for having killed the demon Mahishasura.


Brindavan Gardens

Brindavan Gardens of Mysore is undeniably one of the best terrace gardens in the world. A must sightseeing place of a Mysore tour, Brindavan Garden mesmerizes everyone. It is located about 12km towards the north west of the Mysore city. Brindavan gardens with its illuminated fountains, botanical park, extensive varieties of plants and fulfilled boating, is place for everyone.


Jaganmohan Palace

Jaganmohan Palace, officially Sri Jayachamarajendra Art Gallery, is a royal mansion, arts museum, auditorium, and formerly an alternative royal residence of the ruling maharaja of Mysore. The art gallery contains one of the largest collections of artefacts in South India.


Lalith Mahal

The Lalitha Mahal, now renamed Lalitha Mahal Palace Hotel, is a luxury hotel-turned royal resident and the second largest palace in the southern Indian city of Mysore, Karnataka, after the Mysore Palace. It is located near the Chamundi Hills, east of the city.


St. Philomena’s Cathedral

St. Philomena’s Cathedral is a Catholic church that is the cathedral of the Diocese of Mysore, India. The full name is the Cathedral of St. Joseph and St. Philomena. It is also known as St. Joseph's Cathedral.This is one of the tallest churches in Asia.


Karanji Lake

Karanji Lake is a lake located in the city of Mysore. The lake is surrounded by a nature park consisting of a butterfly park and a walk-through aviary. The aviary constructed on the shore of the lake is the biggest 'walk-through aviary' in India.


Shuka Vana

Home to over 2000 birds, the Shuka Vana in Mysore currently holds the record for most bird species in an aviary. This unique park, commonly known as parrot park, is a part of the Avadhoota Datta Peetham of the Sri Ganapathi Sachidananda Ashram.


Bonsai Garden

The Bonsai Garden of Mysore is home to over a 100 different varieties of Bonsai trees spread across a vast estate of about 4 acres. It is a part of the Avadoota Datta Peetham of the Sri Ganapathi Sachidananda Ashrama.
